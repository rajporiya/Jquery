Explain .fadeIn() and .fadeOut() methods.
    --> The .fadeIn() and .fadeOut() methods are jQuery effects used to show or hide elements with a smooth fading (opacity) animation.

    1. .fadeIn() method:-
        --> The .fadeIn() method gradually makes a hidden element visible by increasing its opacity from 0 to 1.

        Sysntax:-
            $(selector).fadeIn();

    2. .fadeOut() method
        --> The .fadeOut() method gradually hides an element by decreasing its opacity from 1 to 0, then sets display: none.

        Syntacx :- 
            $(selector).fadeOut();


    Key Features

        Works using opacity

        Creates smooth visual transitions

        Accepts:

            "slow", "fast", or milliseconds

            Callback functions

            Improves UI experience
